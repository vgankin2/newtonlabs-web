Cadence — Step Tracker for iPhone and Apple Watch

Frequently asked questions

Cadence isn't showing my steps. What should I check?

Cadence reads step data from Apple Health. Make sure Health access is enabled: go to Settings → Privacy & Security → Health → Cadence and confirm Steps, Walking + Running Distance, and Flights Climbed are all turned on. Then open Cadence and your data should appear.

How do I set separate goals for weekdays and weekends?

Open the Settings tab in Cadence and use the Weekday Goal and Weekend Goal pickers. Cadence automatically applies the right goal each day based on the day of the week.

My step history didn't appear after installing. How do I import it?

On first launch, Cadence shows an import sheet to bring in your Health history. If you dismissed it, you can trigger a re-import by deleting and reinstalling the app. Your iCloud-synced history will restore automatically on the next launch.

Will my data transfer to a new iPhone?

Yes. As long as you are signed into the same iCloud account, Cadence will automatically restore your full history and settings on a new device when you install the app.

How do I get goal notifications?

Cadence sends a notification when you hit your daily step goal. You can also enable an optional morning nudge at 9 AM — find the toggle in Cadence Settings. To turn goal notifications on or off, go to iPhone Settings → Notifications → Cadence.

Does Cadence work with Apple Watch?

Yes. Cadence includes a native Apple Watch app with a 3-page layout showing today's steps and progress, a week chart, and your goal settings. It stays in sync with your iPhone automatically.

Does Cadence collect or sell my data?

No. Cadence does not collect, transmit, or sell any personal or health data. Your data stays on your device and in your private iCloud account. See our Privacy Policy for full details.

What is the tip jar?

Cadence is free to use with no subscriptions. Core step tracking, the Apple Watch app, and all complications will always be free. The tip jar is an optional way to support development if you find the app valuable. Tips are one-time purchases processed securely by Apple.
